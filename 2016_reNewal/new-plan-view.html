          6 Days in South Korea

          한국6일
          South Korea from north to south: Seoul, Sokcho, Busan and Jeju Island.
          We start the first day in South Korea in Seoul, the capital city. Head to the Gyeongbok Palace and explore the grounds taking in the ancient architecture where the Joseon Dynasty lived and reigned. Not wanting to be too far away from tradition, the Blue House, which serves as the president's residency, is right around the corner. After seeing some old Korea, go and explore Myeong-dong, Korea’s vibrant city where the streets are lined with vendors and shops. After eating a delicious dinner at Din Tai Fung, catch a performance by Nanta, a hilarious production similar to STOMP except with kitchen supplies. The next morning wake up early and head to Camp Kim where you will begin your trip to the DMZ and JSA with your USO tour group. Visit the Third Infiltration Tunnel dug by the North Koreans, and then go to the Dora Observatory where you can overlook the North Korean landscape. After that you will go to Dorasan Station, a railway that links to both North and South Korea which is obviously not in use, but is still functional. Finally, you will head to the JSA right on the North and South Korea border where you will see North and South Korean soldiers facing one another and will possibly get to go into one of the conference rooms, actually stepping into North Korea. It’s an intense and awesome experience that you will not want to miss! Once you come back to Seoul in the afternoon, pop over to Itaewon and explore the city that is mostly populated by foreigners from around the world. At night go to Dongdaemun for some late-night discount shopping and possibly see a street performance. On the third day hop on a bus and head to Sokcho, a town in the northeast. After getting settled into your hostel make your way to Naksansa Temple and explore the area for a while, walking along the sea and seeing the large statue of the god/goddess Gwanseum Bosal. After paying your respects to the temple, go and explore Seoraksan National Park where you have quite a few hiking trails to pick from ranging from easier to much harder trails. If hiking isn’t your cup of tea you can easily take the cable car up to the top of one of the mountains and get an incredible view. Wake up early in the morning and go down to the Sokcho fish market and watch the hustle and bustle of the morning fishermen and buyers. After eating some fresh fish, take a bus to the southern end of the country to the second largest city, Busan. After checking into your hotel, get on the subway and check out Shinsegae, the world’s largest department store. After you’ve had your shopping fill, head to Gwangalli beach where you can explore the waterfront and see the Gwangalli Bridge totally lit up at night. Sit at Fuzzy Navel to end your evening drinking fruity cocktails by the glass or by the pitcher. The following morning catch the sunrise at Haedong Yonggungsa and explore the gorgeous Buddhist temple. After that head over to Jagalchi fish market, the largest fish market in South Korea, while it’s still early and watch the vendors with their fresh catches. Cross the street to Nampo-dong and get lost among the different vendors and market people, picking up traditional and not-so-traditional souvenirs. After that it’s time to hop on a quick plane ride to Jeju Island in the southwest. Once there hit up the hilarious theme park called Loveland, which displays all things sexy. Finish your evening by treating yourself to Heukdwaeji, black pig BBQ, an absolute must-eat in Jeju! The best way to get around and see all of Jeju Island is definitely by renting a car. This is generally less expensive in the long run, you can go at your own pace to different attractions, and the view across the island is inspiring. Watch the sun rise at Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak overlooking the massive green crater, and then drive over to Seongeup Folk Village to experience the older architecture, crafts and traditional foods. After that make your way to the Alive Museum where the building is displayed with tons of trick art where you can pose with the awesome photos. Absolutely remember to bring your camera! Finally, make your way back to Jeju City and explore the Dongmun traditional market. For dinner, take advantage of being on the sea and gorge on some delicious grilled seafood caught locally.

          영화 '건축학개론'속 촬영지를 따라서

          한국2일
          영화 '건축학개론' 속 승민과 서연이 되어 떠난 1박 2일 여행
          우리 모두는 누군가의 첫사랑이었다. -영화 '건축학개론' 첫사랑, 처음으로 느끼거나 맺은 사랑. 처음은 언제나 특별하다. 그래서 누구에게나 첫사랑은 잘 잊혀지지 않는, 잊기 힘든 존재다. 누구에게나 똑같이, 그렇게 소중하다. 영화 '건축학개론'은 전국의 많은 이들에게 '첫사랑 신드롬'을 불러 일으켰다. 사람들은 국민여동생 수지와 이제훈의 미소를 보며 자신의 첫사랑을 떠올렸다. 그리고 오랜만에 다시 설렜다. 첫사랑 열풍을 불러일으켰던 영화 '건축학개론' 속 촬영지를 따라 가보기로 한다. 영화 '건축학개론'이 주인공들의 대학 시절 이야기인만큼, 또 이야기가 서울의 한 대학교를 배경으로 펼쳐지는 만큼 실제로 서울 시내에 촬영지가 많았다. 1박2일이면 대중교통을 이용해 쉽게 돌아볼 수 있는 영화 속 장소들. 주인공인 어린 승민과 서연이 대화를 나누고, 서로의 마음을 확인했던 사랑의 장소를 찾아 영화의 장면을 떠올리자. 또 내가 국민여동생 수지는 못돼도, 국민 은지이긴하니, 이번 기회를 통해 우리나라의 명소를 꼼꼼하게 돌아보자. '건축학개론' 촬영지를 따라 승민과 서연의 흔적을 찾아가는 길에 나도 나의 첫사랑을 떠올릴 수 있길. 그리고 고마워 할 수있길 바라며.
